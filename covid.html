COVID-19 Crisis

Our response to the global pandemic

LIVING IN LOCKDOWN

We are grateful to report that we have not had any cases of COVID-19 on our campus.
Our staff and children are locked down, safe behind our gates. We are taking every
recaution we can to keep them safe. None of us have ever seen a moment in time
like this: A single virus has united billions of us with the same struggles,
he same isolation, the same fears and concerns. Yet somehow we are finding
life and hope in it all.

    The key areas that the virus is affecting us here on the mission are:

  • The return of dozens of past children means more mouths to feed but also more hands to help.
  • A loss of self-generated funding due to the closing of our school has made paying salaries and wages a real struggle for us.
  • Closed borders have meant a lack of laborers for the completion of our farm work.
  • Unavailability of resources limits where we are able to help
  • Our school has become a quarantine center for anyone coming in from other states or districts.
  • We have been forced to close our volunteer program for the foreseeable future.

THE PROBLEMS WE ARE FACING

Everyone across the globe is struggling with Corona virus in
their own way. These are the key areas that we are in need at
the moment:

School Closed

To help prevent the spread of
the disease, the Government
has closed down all schools
across our nation. This means
additional responsibility now
falls on the shoulders of our
staff here at home.

Low Finances

With our school shut, fees
used to pay our staff and
admin expenses are not
coming in. For the first time in
more than a decade we are
now 100% reliant on
donations to continue to
survive.

Farm Workers

Our farm relies on seasonal
labor from another district.
With travel restrictions in
place, we are struggling to
ensure crops are planted and
cared for appropriately.

Quarantine

We have offered our empty
school to the Government to
be used as a quarantine
center. Extra resources are
needed to support those
isolated within it’s gates.
